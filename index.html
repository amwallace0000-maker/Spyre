05 / Understanding the Buyer
Designing For The Buyer

Who will actually buy here.

Today's purchaser is not comparing apartments against apartments. They are comparing apartment living against the home they already own. The project must give them compelling reasons to move.

Segment 01

Affluent downsizers

The largest and most important segment. Typically 55 to 75, often selling waterfront homes of $6M to $30M. They are not downsizing, they are upgrading their lifestyle: simplified living, security, travel flexibility, wellness, lock-and-leave.

Segment 02

Business owners & executives

A luxury primary residence or city escape. They value privacy, security, concierge, home-office capability and premium entertaining. Many operate businesses well into retirement and work remotely.

Segment 03

Second-home purchasers

Predominantly Brisbane, Sydney and Melbourne, seeking premium low-maintenance coastal residences. Convenience becomes paramount; quality becomes non-negotiable.

Segment 04

High-net-worth retirees

Purchasing without finance, deciding on lifestyle rather than return. Service, community and convenience dominate the decision.

Geographic profile

Where demand comes from.

  • South East Queensland: primarily Gold Coast owner occupiers and Brisbane second-home purchasers
  • Sydney & Melbourne: lifestyle-driven buyers seeking a luxury coastal residence
  • New Zealand: high-net-worth purchasers with an established affinity for the Gold Coast lifestyle
  • Selective international markets: subject to prevailing foreign investment regulations
Purchasing motivation

What they are actually buying.

  • Simplified luxury living
  • Premium lifestyle amenity
  • Security and convenience
  • Architectural quality
  • Prestige location
  • Long-term capital preservation
  • Lock-and-leave ownership

These buyers are not simply purchasing an apartment. They are investing in a lifestyle that reflects their aspirations and stage of life.

While investor demand will remain important, we believe owner occupiers and second-home purchasers will ultimately underpin pricing and long-term project success, especially given recent legislative changes impacting investors.

Buyer alignment

Not every residence within the development needs to target the same purchaser. Premium eastern residences should primarily appeal to owner occupiers and second-home buyers seeking larger floorplans, premium views and an elevated lifestyle experience. Conversely, smaller residences and more accessible price points will continue to appeal to investors and lifestyle purchasers seeking flexibility, location, lifestyle and value. Designing the product mix around these buyer profiles creates a more balanced offering while strengthening pricing across the development.

Two principles

Living should always face the view.

The most valuable outlook belongs to the spaces where residents spend their day: living, dining, kitchen and outdoor entertaining, ahead of bedrooms, bathrooms and utility. The emotional connection that supports pricing is formed there.

Rethinking four bedrooms

Flexibility beats bedroom count.

Many downsizers no longer need four permanent bedrooms but highly value flexibility. Consider three bedrooms plus a multipurpose room, executive office, library, media room, wellness room or flexible guest accommodation, whilst maintaining a four-bedroom option as demand requires it.

06 / East Tower · Design Recommendations
The East Tower

A boutique residential address.

The current design provides a strong foundation. Several refinements can significantly improve the resident experience while supporting stronger pricing, increased buyer demand and enhanced gross realisation.

The proposed amenity for the East Tower provides a solid foundation; however, we believe it falls short of the expectations of today's apartment purchaser, which when met greatly improves yield. Rather than maximising apartment numbers, consideration should be given to creating a premium wellness precinct that becomes one of the defining features of the development. Each recommendation is tied to a plan. Open the plan to see the specific change in context.

Resident amenity & wellness

Levels 2 & 3

Destination wellness precinct

Replace the two apartments on Level 2 and the two on Level 3 with premium resident amenity, and dedicate the floors to a world-class wellness destination.

Wellness

A gym with natural light

Expand the gym to approximately 250 to 300 m² with abundant natural light. Current designs appear to have no natural light at all. Add a dedicated Pilates reformer studio and a massage and treatment room.

Wellness

Water & heat therapy

Magnesium hot and cold plunge pools, with separate steam rooms and saunas, including a dedicated ladies' wellness retreat where practical. This influences purchasing decisions with the older female demographic.

Wellness

Recovery & advanced therapy

Consider additional premium wellness experiences such as a red-light therapy bed, a cryochamber or snow room, and dedicated recovery facilities.

Level 2

Residents' lounge & private dining

A residents' lounge incorporating a private dining room, entertaining kitchen and direct outdoor connection where possible, so residents entertain larger groups without oversized apartments.

Commercial benefit

Four lots, and what they buy

Removing four apartments reduces net saleable area, but the value created through premium resident amenity outweighs the value of four apartments with limited views that look west into Tower 2, which constrains the rates achievable on them.

Concierge & resident services

The arrival experience establishes a buyer's first impression of the development and should reflect the calibre of the project. Today's luxury apartment purchaser increasingly expects a service offering comparable to premium hotels.

Ground floor

Concierge & building management

A dedicated concierge reception and building management office: visitor assistance, resident support and contractor coordination. The operational heart of the building and a premium first impression.

Ground floor

Executive meeting room

An executive meeting room with full audio-visual capability, plus a resident business lounge where practical: business meetings, client presentations, board and family-office discussions, without external venues.

Ground floor

Parcel & grocery storage

Secure parcel management and refrigerated grocery storage, so online grocery and meal deliveries are accepted whether or not residents are home. Inexpensive to provide, exceptional in convenience.

Premium resident services reinforce the luxury positioning of the project while improving convenience, operational efficiency and long-term resident satisfaction.

Car parking strategy

Parking allocation remains one of the most influential purchasing considerations within this buyer segment. Our experience indicates that owner occupiers purchasing at this level generally expect three secure car parks, whilst second-home purchasers will often accept two. Additional car spaces significantly impact salability and rates achieved: an additional car park will typically achieve around $250,000 if sold individually, but the yield is significantly higher when incorporated into the apartment offering.

Recommendations

Protect long-term value.

  • Utilise opportunities created through lower-level redesign to increase parking allocation
  • Investigate the potential to deliver an additional 10 to 20 spaces where practical
  • Prioritise three-car allocations across premium owner-occupier residences
Commercial benefit

Fewer objections, higher rates.

Improved parking ratios will strengthen owner-occupier demand, support higher pricing and reduce objections throughout the sales process.

Apartment design optimisation

Whilst the existing layouts provide a solid foundation, thoughtful refinements have the potential to significantly improve functionality and buyer appeal.

Reorient kitchens and primary living areas towards uninterrupted eastern views wherever practical.

Three bedrooms plus MPR

Review four-bedroom layouts in favour of three-bedroom plus multi-purpose room configurations where appropriate, whilst maintaining a four-bedroom option as demand requires it.

Master suites

Maximise premium frontage

Reassess master suite layouts to maximise premium frontage, with improved storage and private lift lobbies where practical.

Structure

Review blade elements

Review architectural blade elements that may compromise premium view corridors to the east.

Sub-Penthouses

Differentiate the top

Further differentiate Sub-Penthouses through upgraded finishes, specifications and ceiling heights.

Penthouses

Homes, not apartments

Incorporate internal lifts, rooftop kitchens, outdoor entertaining areas and priority lift access within Penthouse residences.

Premium specifications

The project's specification schedule should continue to be benchmarked against competing luxury developments to ensure it meets or exceeds buyer expectations. Consideration should be given to premium appliance packages, smart home integration, bespoke joinery, lighting design, ceiling heights and material selections, particularly across the premium residences, Sub-Penthouses and Penthouses. We can provide a baseline outlining what competing products are offering to ensure these minimum standards are met.

View corridors

One of the East Tower's greatest competitive advantages is its uninterrupted eastern outlook. Wherever practical, apartment layouts should prioritise eastern-facing living, dining and entertaining spaces to maximise buyer appeal and fully capitalise on the project's premium beachfront position, instead of being oriented north and south into neighbouring buildings. Orientating the primary living experience towards these view corridors will strengthen both buyer perception and long-term value.

Positioned as Main Beach's premier boutique residential address, the East Tower should compete with the very best boutique developments on the Gold Coast. Thoughtful refinement of the resident amenity, apartment layouts, parking allocation and premium specifications presents an opportunity to create a significantly more desirable product without fundamentally changing the vision of the development. These recommendations are not intended to increase apartment numbers. They are intended to increase buyer aspiration.

07 / Tower 2 · The Sky Collection
Above The East Tower

The upper levels, a distinct collection.

Tower 2 presents the single greatest opportunity to further strengthen buyer appeal, maximise pricing and salability, and maximise overall gross realisation.

Whilst the current design provides a strong foundation, the upper levels continue to adopt a largely consistent floor plate despite a significant improvement in outlook once the building rises above the East Tower. From a buyer's perspective, these upper levels represent a fundamentally different product and should be designed, positioned and priced accordingly. Reducing density within the building will also support better livability, improved car parking ratios and less demand on amenity.

Product hierarchy

Rather than continuing with a consistent apartment offering throughout the building, we recommend creating a progressive hierarchy of residences that become increasingly exclusive as elevation increases, reflected through apartment size, specifications, ceiling heights, parking allocation, resident experience and ultimately pricing.

The transition

The Sky Collection

Once Tower 2 rises above the East Tower roofline, framed views become uninterrupted panoramic ocean views. This is not a better apartment; it is a different product. Reconfigure the upper 12 to 14 levels as a distinct collection of Sky Residences above the standard apartment product.

Frontage

Wider to the east

Introduce larger premium residences with wider eastern frontage taking advantage of the uninterrupted eastern views, with increased internal living and outdoor entertaining areas.

Specification

A different calibre of finish

Introduce upgraded specifications and finishes: ceiling heights lifted to 3 to 3.5 m, bespoke joinery, upgraded appliances, enhanced natural stone, home automation, premium audio, heated flooring and towel rails, wine storage and outdoor kitchens.

Owner-occupier

Purpose-built for downsizers

Buyers transitioning from 500 to 1,000 m² homes will not compromise on living space, entertaining, storage, privacy, parking or quality. Design these levels specifically for them.

Servicing

Six lifts and storage

Increase the number of lifts servicing the tower to six, and provide resident storage that is currently absent from the core, with private lift lobbies where practical.

Parking

Three-car allocation

Improve parking allocations across premium levels. Parking is among the most common prestige objections. Target three secure spaces wherever practical across the premium owner-occupier product.

Creating a clearly defined product hierarchy strengthens buyer aspiration, supports higher rates per m² and provides greater flexibility when managing pricing throughout the sell-down.

Apartment mix

Our experience indicates the strongest depth of demand sits within residences of approximately 200 m² to 300 m², particularly amongst downsizers, owner occupiers and second-home purchasers seeking generous living without the ongoing maintenance associated with larger waterfront homes. Whilst larger residences will remain important, this size range consistently represents the strongest balance between buyer demand, pricing and absorption.

Recommendations

Fewer apartments, more value.

  • Introduce larger 202 to 214 m² residences throughout selected premium levels
  • Consider six residences per floor by combining existing apartments
  • Transition to four, and ultimately two, residences per floor across the uppermost levels
  • Retain the flexibility to create a full-floor penthouse should market conditions support it
  • Reduce apartment numbers in favour of higher-value product
Commercial benefit

Aligned to real demand.

This product mix better aligns with current buyer demand while maintaining stronger rates per m² across the premium levels of the development.

Premium view corridors

One of Tower 2's greatest competitive advantages is the uninterrupted eastern outlook achieved once the building rises above the East Tower, and its Broadwater views to the north once it clears neighbouring buildings. Apartment layouts should maximise these premium view corridors by prioritising living, dining and entertaining spaces centred around privacy and views.

From a buyer's perspective, there is a significant psychological difference between looking between buildings and looking over them. Once these uninterrupted view corridors are achieved, buyers perceive the product as fundamentally different and are willing to pay accordingly. The current matrix indicates limited premium east-facing product despite this significant advantage. We believe this presents an opportunity to redesign the upper levels to maximise direct eastern frontage, creating larger owner-occupier residences capable of commanding materially stronger rates per m².

Lift strategy

With approximately 280 apartments proposed within Tower 2, lift performance will become an important component of the overall resident experience. An allowance of only three lifts to service this volume of apartments will significantly impact sales velocity and rates, as well as livability.

Recommendations

Six lifts, zoned.

  • Investigate opportunities to increase lift capacity to a minimum of six
  • Consider programming lifts to service separate sections of the building, levels 5 to 22 and 23 to 34 as an example
  • Provide priority lift access for premium two-per-floor and whole-floor residences
  • Investigate dedicated service lift requirements should holiday letting be incorporated
Commercial benefit

Servicing that matches the price point.

Improved lift functionality enhances convenience, reduces wait times and better aligns the building's operation with buyer expectations at this end of the market. Lift capacity should also be considered in the context of move-ins, move-outs, servicing requirements and any future holiday letting operations, to ensure long-term functionality supports the project's premium positioning.

Holiday letting considerations

Clarification is required regarding the intended holiday letting strategy, as this will influence the design, sales rates and long-term operation of the building. Our recommendation is that holiday letting is not encouraged. Should it form part of the overall project strategy, consideration should be given to:

Open item for Spyre

Design and operational implications.

  • Dedicated service lift requirements
  • Housekeeping operations
  • Lift capacity during peak check-in and departure periods
  • Concierge and building management functions
  • Appropriate Body Corporate by-laws
  • Separation of owner-occupier and investor product where practical, limiting holiday letting to levels 5 to 22 as an example
Why it matters now

Decide before design locks.

Thoughtful planning at this stage will help preserve the resident experience while ensuring the building operates efficiently over the long term. Resolved late, it becomes a retrofit; resolved now, it costs nothing.

Reducing apartment numbers on premium levels may appear to reduce gross realisation. Our view is the opposite: larger residences with uninterrupted eastern views achieve materially higher rates per m², and combined with scarcity, parking and specification, the pricing more than offsets the reduction in count. Rather than simply repeating apartment layouts as the building rises, the product should evolve to reflect the increasing value of elevation, outlook and exclusivity.

Controlled sell-down strategy

One of the greatest opportunities to maximise gross realisation lies in the management of inventory throughout the sales program. Rather than releasing all product simultaneously, we recommend a disciplined release strategy that allows pricing to respond to buyer demand, market conditions and sales momentum. Our experience has consistently demonstrated that disciplined inventory management delivers stronger commercial outcomes than attempting to maximise launch volumes.

Benefits of the approach

Scarcity, staged.

  • Maintaining scarcity
  • Protecting pricing integrity
  • Allowing future releases to benefit from established sales evidence
  • Reducing unnecessary discounting
  • Supporting stronger project valuation outcomes
Release discipline

In practice.

  • Hold back selected premium residences
  • Release premium inventory progressively
  • Adjust release timing to demand, not fixed dates
  • Let premium product appreciate faster than standard

Measuring success

Beyond total sales

The metrics that matter.

  • Gross realisation and rate per internal and total m²
  • Sales velocity and contract conversion
  • Days from enquiry to contract
  • Price growth achieved between releases
Summary

Design, strategy, execution.

Disciplined pricing, controlled inventory management and a relationship-led sales strategy provide the strongest platform to maximise returns for stakeholders while protecting pricing integrity throughout the campaign. Value should be created through design, protected through strategy and realised through disciplined execution.

11 / Sales Strategy
A Disciplined Sell-Down

Enquiry to contract.

The challenge is not generating enquiry. It is converting enquiry into qualified contracts while carefully managing remaining inventory.

The sales strategy should be aligned with the hierarchy of the product. Premium owner-occupier residences should be targeted through direct relationships and private engagement, while investor product can be strategically introduced through local marketing and selected channel partners where appropriate. This approach protects premium stock while ensuring every buyer segment is appropriately serviced.

Chairman's List

Before any public launch, a private list of the highest-quality buyers known to Spyre, Kollosche and Place. Private briefings and early access build emotional commitment before contracts issue.

Database qualification

12 / Launch Strategy

Demand before launch.
